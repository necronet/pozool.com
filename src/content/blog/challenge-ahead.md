Pozool Stays is projected as a new solution among an ocean of alternatives, and as the new player on the field, there are many challenges ahead. Let’s list some technical and business-related ones (because it’s always fun to know what’s coming).

## OTA API's the Iron Curtain

The short-term rental industry such as Airbnb, Booking, VRBO and others follows an agency model, officially called an Online Travel Agency (OTA). They act as intermediaries, building sophisticated systems that orchestrate payments, identity verification, search algorithms, navigate local regulations in each country, etc. All with the objective of obtaining a large amount of demand and listing inventory. As a reward for acting as an intermediary between supply and demand, a service commission per transaction is charged to both parties, or [only one](https://www.airbnb.com/resources/hosting-homes/a/simplifying-airbnb-service-fees-746). Here lies the first challenge of building a PMS whose participants (host, landlord, renter) are connected to these platforms, which protect information behind a high wall, jealous of who can have access to this information. So the first friction point is: how do you obtain this information if you are not an important player in the game? Important players in the industry gain access through Partner programs ([Booking Partner](https://join.booking.com/index.html), [Airbnb Partner](https://www.airbnb.com/software-partners)). These accesses facilitate the construction of a channel manager that can synchronize information into a single point; the direct benefit is coverage.

#### Are we then limited from building a PMS without having the capital or presence?

There is then a chicken-and-egg problem. Pozool Stays is not large or important enough to gain access to partner programs, but it is flexible enough to focus on other points that small and medium operators will be interested in. The key focus of Stays is to facilitate the administration of real estate portfolios whose nature is a mix of traditional long-term rentals (6 months - years) and short-term rentals (daily and weekly), with a lightweight layer of connection with OTAs.

## Resisting the impulses in a world of AI

The world has changed, and SaaS is a different game today than it was two years ago. [The SaaSpocalypse is the red alert](https://capital-riesgo.es/es/articles/el-saas-no-est-muerto-pero-tampoco-igual-que-antes-) for every company dedicated to offering software as a key product. That is why the moment to create a PMS for operators who have historically been relegated to Excel sheets and manual controls has arrived. Much of the challenge now becomes knowing how to choose what is necessary and what matters less. Every capability has earned its place in Stays; it is not there for decoration or ego injection, and each of them will constantly be evaluated to determine its usefulness in operations.

So it is guaranteed that in the near future of this year, AI will play a role in creation, but also within the operation of the system as an assistant. Here are some of the rules that have been put in place to avoid “shitifying” the product:

* The use of the assistant within the platform will be strictly evaluated and grounded in real data, with a limited scope to avoid hallucinating numbers in a “creative” way.
* Operational control will always remain in the hands of business administrators. Every real estate business can only truly exist in the real world; it is a physical business with an online presence to operate, not a digital business with a physical presence to fulfill operations.

## Monetization and sustainability

One of the most common mistakes when building software is thinking the product exists only to grow users, when in reality the objective is to create a sustainable business. Real estate software is not immune to this. The average operator does not want a platform with one hundred buttons and futuristic dashboards; they want to solve concrete problems: avoid overbookings, know how much money is coming in, control maintenance, organize cleaning, understand how much a property actually generates, and reduce operational chaos, and they would like to perform these activities at a price that is reasonable and easy to justify.

Given the scale of the operation, and because we like to keep our feet on the ground, the final objective is not to compete with giant established PMS platforms but rather to have an almost surgical focus on solving the most important problems for operators who are struggling to manage their portfolios in the traditional way. We will be happy if we:

* Automate repetitive tasks that consume time.
* Centralize financial and operational information.
* Reduce dependency on scattered manual processes.
* Keep infrastructure costs relatively low to avoid passing absurd prices onto operators.

So there is still no price for Pozool Stays; at this stage we continue evaluating what our place in the market is. Soon we will have the opportunity to share a live demo so it can be used as a guide and to know whether this is something people are interested in.

## User acquisition strategy

Pozool Stays will probably not gain users initially by spending millions on ads or trying to dominate Google results from day one. We expect traction to happen in a more organic, simple, and economically viable way. So we will remain local, working with real operators, with constant and early feedback, optimizing and automating processes. In the short term, acquisition will probably move around:

* Local operator communities.
* Organic networking and direct relationships.
* Technical and operational content.
* Word-of-mouth recommendations.
